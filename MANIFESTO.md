# The criteria Manifesto

*[Versión en español →](./MANIFESTO.es.md)*

**criteria** is an open format — and a reference engine — for **human
criterion**: the practical wisdom of applying good judgment to concrete
situations.

## 1. Human-first, AI-optional

The deletion test: **if you remove the AI, criteria still works.** A person
with a form and a folder of JSON files has a full decision journal, a track
record, and a community of criterion. AI is optional plumbing — it may
transcribe, structure, and retrieve. It never judges.

## 2. Authority flows from humans

In an AI skill, the model holds the judgment and the skill steers it. Here it
is reversed: **humans are the source of truth**. The engine retrieves,
aggregates and returns what humans poured into it — always with provenance,
never from a model's imagination.

## 3. You own your criterion

Personal criterion is **private, local-first and portable**. It lives on your
machine, in plain files, under no vendor. Publishing it is always an explicit
act, never a default.

## 4. Criterion is layered and federated

Community criterion is shared, versioned and forkable — like code. Personal
criterion inherits from it and overrides it. A domain (medicine, carpentry,
software) is just a folder anyone can create.

## 5. Judgment looks through lenses, not databases

Humans do not scan everything they know. They glance at the **outer layers** —
a handful of points of view — pick the ones that matter, and weigh them. The
format stores exactly that: lenses, declared weights, residual doubt. Nothing
deeper, because nothing deeper is how real judgment works.

## 6. Weights are earned, not configured

A lens gains weight in a domain because it repeatedly mattered in real
decisions **that turned out well**. Expectation vs outcome — the closed loop —
is what separates criterion from opinion.

## 7. Doubt is data

The small doubt that remains after deciding is recorded, not hidden. Confidence
is always reported. An engine that never says *"I don't know — teach me"* is
not carrying criterion; it is faking it.
